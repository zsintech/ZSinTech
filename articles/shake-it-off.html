Shake it Off

Ancient wisdom for modern transitions

Seisachtheia—a word recently occupying my thoughts—came from my Ancient Greeks class, meaning "shaking off burdens." Historically, Athens had debtor classes called Hectomers who owed agricultural products to elite creditors. Failure to repay resulted in self-enslavement. Solon, an Athenian lawmaker, pardoned these debts (with creditors' reluctant consent) and prohibited enslaving Athenian citizens. However, the ownership of foreign slaves persisted.

My fascination lies in the term itself, not a commendation of ancient Greek morality, which was flawed in its "holier-than-thou" manner. Nevertheless, the beauty of language in having a word like Seisachtheia captivated me.

A similar concept resonates deeply within my current transitional life phase, as I've shed various burdens, embracing new challenges without prior paralysis. Rather than feeling overwhelmed, I am pacing myself thoughtfully—a significant personal achievement. This introspection marks monumental personal growth, teaching me to appreciate and slowly embrace life's unfolding journey.
